Curveball Cards

GTM AI Workshop · LearnLoop · Drop one on a team ~60–90 min in, when the build-slump hits. Each is a message "from LearnLoop's VP GTM." Read it out loud for maximum chaos.

Any team — pick by the room's energy
01 Any team

"Board meeting just got moved up. I need something I can show in 20 minutes — not explain. Make it visual, now." — VP GTM

Why: forces a demoable artifact over a perfect one. Kills over-engineering exactly when it creeps in.

02 Any team

"Legal killed our boldest claim. Whatever you're building can no longer rely on your single strongest assumption. Adapt." — VP GTM

Why: real GTM whiplash. Rewards teams whose build is flexible, not brittle.

03 Any team

"Our biggest competitor just shipped the exact thing you're building. So — what makes ours different? Bake it in before the demo." — VP GTM

Why: pushes differentiation and sharper positioning under time pressure.

04 Any team

"Budget's frozen. Your solution now has to run on tools LearnLoop already has — no new spend. Show me it still works." — VP GTM

Why: constraint = creativity. Mirrors the real "make it work with what we've got."

Brief 01 — Account penetration
05 Brief 01

"Our #1 target account just got acquired. Half your list's firmographics are now wrong. How does your machine handle stale data?" — VP GTM

Why: tests whether the "machine" is robust or a one-shot demo. Great teams handle it gracefully.

06 Brief 01

"The CHRO you were about to email just posted: 'if one more vendor sends me a personalised-but-obviously-AI email…' — go on. Beat that." — VP GTM

Why: raises the bar past "mail-merge with AI" toward genuinely human, relevant outreach.

Brief 02 — Brand voice
07 Brief 02

"Marketing lead quit. A brand-new hire publishes tomorrow and has never read the voice guide. Your system has to catch them cold." — VP GTM

Why: the whole point of the brief — does the system hold without the humans who wrote it?

08 Brief 02

"Sales says the German market finds our voice 'too casual to trust.' Prove your localisation actually handles that — don't just translate it." — VP GTM

Why: forces adapt-don't-translate, the trap baked into the localisation pieces.

Brief 03 — Signal room
09 Brief 03

"The CEO only reads one number. Which single metric do you put in front of her — and why that one?" — VP GTM

Why: forces synthesis over dashboards. Separates "here's all the data" from "here's what matters."

10 Brief 03

"Confession: one of those data sources is partly fake — a test import nobody cleaned. Can your synthesis survive bad data?" — VP GTM

Why: rewards teams who question their inputs instead of trusting the pile (there really is a planted bug).

Wildcards — use sparingly, for the lols
W1 Wildcard

"Swap one teammate with the table next to you for 10 minutes. Fresh eyes on both builds." — The Board

Why: breaks tunnel vision, sparks cross-pollination, and it's just fun to watch.

W2 Wildcard

"Speed round. 15 minutes to get ONE thing working end-to-end. Ring the bell when it does. Go." — The Board

Why: injects urgency mid-build and drives bell-rings (energy on the leaderboard).

All scenarios fictional — LearnLoop isn't real. Facilitator tip: don't hand every card to every team. Read the room, pick 1–2 per team, and time them for the mid-build lull. Save the "Any team" cards for a synchronised drop across the whole room if energy dips at once.
