## Brief 01 — The signal room
**Owner (prep):** TBD

Analytics, funnel drop-off, reviews, LinkedIn engagement — LearnLoop is drowning in data and nobody can say what it means. **The pile is messy on purpose.**

**The build (v4):** the signal triage — identify and prioritize accounts: which of the 50 is hot, which is not, why, who to contact right now, and with what hook. The messy internal data pile calibrates what "hot for LearnLoop" means. Not a dashboard.

**Data pack needs:**
- Messy analytics export (funnel stages with drop-off, deliberately noisy)
- Review corpus (G2/Capterra-style, mixed sentiment)
- LinkedIn engagement export (posts, reactions, comments)
- Deliberate contradictions/red herrings baked in
- No pre-cleaned "answer" — the mess is the point

## Brief 02 — The brand voice machine
**Owner (prep):** TBD

Content output 4x'd this year, six people now publish in the company's name, and the brand voice is drifting into beige. Now LearnLoop is expanding to **Spain and Germany** too. Hand-editing doesn't scale.

**The build (v4):** the content production line — asset brief in, on-voice asset out, across authors, formats, markets, and languages (EN → DE/ES, extensible to any). Assume the accounts are already picked (Brief 01's job). The codified voice + check/rewrite loop is its quality gate.

**Data pack needs:**
- LearnLoop brand voice guide (POVs, tone rules, banned phrases)
- 6 sample pieces from 6 "authors" showing the drift (some on-voice, some beige)
- 2–3 pieces needing DE/ES localisation (not just translation — transcreation)
- A "gold standard" reference piece

## Brief 03 — The account penetration machine
**Owner (prep):** Casper + Twan

Five enterprise logos wanted from 50 DACH accounts this quarter. Current outbound reply rate: **0.4%**. The list is being burned with templates nobody answers.

**The build (v4):** the motion machine — a prioritized account + signal in (Brief 01's kind of output), the play out: channel routing (email, LinkedIn, phone, references, even physical gifts), buying-committee orchestration, timing, and asset briefs for Brief 02 to produce. Signal capture belongs to Brief 01; copywriting to Brief 02.

**Data pack needs:**
- 50 fictional DACH target accounts (company, size, industry, tech signals)
- Contact map per account (2–4 personas: economic buyer, champion, blocker)
- The current burned templates (so teams see what *not* to do)
- Reply-rate baseline + a "what good looks like" target
- Optional: fake intent/trigger signals (job posts, funding, tech installs)
