## The company

LearnLoop is a B2B learning platform for the mid-market, founded 2019 in Amsterdam. One promise since day one: **close the loop between learning and doing.** Most learning tools prove your people finished the training. LearnLoop proves the skill landed.

| | |
|---|---|
| HQ | Amsterdam (office in Berlin) |
| Founded | 2019 |
| Funding | Series C, €45M (late 2025); €68M total |
| Team | ~280 people |
| ARR | **€30M**, growing ~55% (down from 90% two years ago) |
| Customers | ~750 mid-market companies (200 to 2,000 employees), deals of €1.3k to €10k MRR |
| Home markets | Benelux; DACH beachhead (Berlin office, first logos won warm) |
| Expansion | **DACH: from beachhead to second home market. Spain next.** |
| Board plan | **€200M ARR by 2030** |

## The product

Three modules, one loop:

- **Learn** — the LMS. Courses, paths, compliance tracking, mobile.
- **Loop** — the skills engine. Maps who can actually do what, flags the gaps, prescribes the learning that closes them. This is the moat, and the thing customers rave about.
- **Market** — 8,000+ courses from external providers, bought and administered in one place.

Why customers buy: they have been burned before. ◆86% of HR leaders say they cannot clearly see the skills their organisation has today (LinkedIn talent research). ◆People forget most of what they learn within a week unless it is reinforced (the Ebbinghaus curve). A completion certificate measures attendance, not capability. LearnLoop sells the receipts.

Reskilling moved from HR project to board topic. ◆The WEF expects 59 of every 100 workers to need reskilling or upskilling by 2030, and 39% of core skills to change. ◆The European learning-platform market is roughly $7B and growing ~20% a year. Crowded, too:

## The moment

This is not a turnaround story. The product wins deals. Customers renew. The pitch lands. Even the DACH beachhead is real: a Berlin office and the first German logos, won the artisanal way — founder intros, warm network, feet on the ground. The board looked at all of that and wrote a bigger number: €200M by 2030. And one proof point this quarter: show that the warm-network beachhead can become a *system*. Germany first.

## The wall

Growth is fine. The **cost** of growth is the problem. Every euro of new pipeline still scales with headcount:

- **One** RevOps analyst reads four data sources. Nobody agrees what the numbers mean, so every meeting re-litigates them.
- **Six** writers publish under the brand. The voice is held together by hand-editing, one draft at a time. Next quarter it also has to work in German and Spanish.
- Campaign personalisation is artisanal, so once the warm network ran dry, the 3-person Berlin pod fell back to templates: **3,200 emails, 13 replies. 0.4%.**

Watch any great marketer or SDR work and you see three skills: they **sense** (who is worth it, why now), they **speak** (what to say, to which persona, in whose voice), and they **reach** (land it, thread the account, earn the reply). All three run from someone's head. Heads do not multiply. Systems do. And hiring six times the team is not a plan; it is a payroll problem with a ramp-time problem on top.

LearnLoop knows its market, its ICP, and its pitch. This is not a strategy question. It is a documentation, data, and engineering question: **we know what to do and why. We do not have the system to do it at scale.**

## The mission — today

You are LearnLoop's **marketing engineering task force**. Three subsystems, one machine:

| Brief | Subsystem | You build |
|---|---|---|
| The Signal Room | **SENSE** | The layer that turns four messy data sources into "what it means and what to do" |
| The Brand Voice Machine | **SPEAK** | The system that holds the voice across six authors and three languages |
| The Account Penetration Machine | **REACH** | The workflow that turns research and live signals into outreach that earns replies |

Proving ground: **50 named DACH accounts. 5 logos this quarter. A 3-person pod. Zero new hires.**

We are not automating the conversation. We are automating everything that stands *between* marketing and a booked conversation. The people keep the judgment and the talking. The machine does the rest.

## The finale — close the loop

LearnLoop's product closes the loop between learning and doing. Its own pipeline machine runs open-loop: nothing it does feeds back into what it knows. In the last half hour, the three subsystems reconnect: **SENSE finds the play. SPEAK arms it. REACH lands it. The replies flow back.** If one account can travel the whole machine, LearnLoop just learned a new market.

Close the loop.
