## 1. WIN — Van Rooijen Retail Groep B.V. (NL, mid-market retail)

**Date:** 11 Feb 2026 · **Deal size:** €48k ACV (Learn+Loop) · **Attendees:** Rick Tervoort (L&D Manager, champion), Marieke Aalders (VP HR, EB — joined final call only), Noor Willemsen (LearnLoop AE)

Rick ran most of this solo for the first month, classic champion deal. He'd been burned before — had Cognita on trial for content but said it never told him anything about actual competence, just who clicked through what. Two demos, one with Marieke sitting in near the end because Rick needed her sign-off on the seat count (900 across store ops + HQ). She asked sharp questions about the gap report specifically, not about the course library at all, which honestly made the rest of the call easy. Procurement was a non-event, standard DPA, nothing weird. Rick kept pushing back internally on the Cognita renewal the whole cycle, said out loud in the second call that "a library is not the problem I have." Closed faster than our average NL cycle, ~6 weeks.

**Buyer quotes:**
- "Cognita gives me a library. I already have a library — SharePoint's full of PDFs nobody opens." — Rick Tervoort
- "The thing that actually got Marieke's attention was the gap report, not the course catalogue." — Rick Tervoort
- "I don't need another dashboard. I need to know if the floor managers can actually run a shift." — Marieke Aalders

**Next steps:** Signed 20 Feb. CS handoff done, kickoff week of 2 Mar. Rick flagged a second business unit (~150 seats) for H2 — worth a check-in in Q3.

## 2. WIN — Falkenrode Logistik GmbH (DACH beachhead, Hamburg)

**Date:** 4 Mar 2026 · **Deal size:** €72k ACV · **Attendees:** Torsten Ahlers (Head of L&D, EB), Daan Vermeulen (LearnLoop CEO — founder intro), Bram Hoekstra (CRO), Finn Bergmann (AE)

This is the one everyone wanted on the call for. Daan knows Torsten from way back (former colleague, reconnected at a logistics conference last year) and made the intro personally — Finn picked it up from there and ran the actual deal, Bram sat in on the final call given how strategic this logo is for the DACH story. Landed exactly on his pain point (1,100 people, safety procedures, zero visibility into who actually knows the new process vs. who clicked "complete").

**Buyer quotes:**

- "Someone forwarded me your completion-metrics post — that is exactly our problem. We have completion rates everywhere and nobody can tell me if the warehouse team actually knows the new safety procedure."
- "Once I trust the source, procurement is basically a formality."

**Next steps:** Signed. Onboarding running with the Berlin pod. Torsten open to a reference call for future DACH prospects — worth pursuing for the account penetration work.

## 3. LOSS — Rehbach Präzisionswerke GmbH (DE, mid-market manufacturer)

**Date:** 22 Apr 2026 · **Deal size:** ~€65k ACV (proposed, lost) · **Attendees:** Jonas Feldmann (L&D Manager, champion), Ute Grabowski (Betriebsrat / works council), procurement (name not captured), Finn Bergmann (AE), Lukas Brandt (Berlin pod, sourced)

Painful one, had good momentum through the first two calls. Jonas was genuinely sold on Loop, but this fell apart on two fronts at once. First, switching cost — they're deep into a SkillForge integration (SSO, HR system, two years of setup) and ripping that out was its own project before we even entered the picture. Second, and this is the one that actually killed it: the works council flagged that Loop's skill-mapping — tracking who can do what, by name — reads as employee monitoring under German co-determination rules. Jonas tried to reframe it as a coaching tool, didn't land. Procurement then slow-walked everything once the works council objection was on the table, and by mid-April it was clear this wasn't moving. They're renewing SkillForge.

**Buyer quotes:**
- "Our Betriebsrat will not sign off on anything that looks like it profiles individual employees, and Loop maps who can do what by name."
- "We already sank two years into SkillForge integrations. Ripping that out is its own project before we even get to yours."
- "This is not compliance training, this is a performance file, and my colleagues will read it that way." — Ute Grabowski

**Next steps:** None — closed lost. Rep note: flag to product — an aggregate-only / anonymised reporting mode might unblock works-council-sensitive DE deals. Revisit in 12 months.

## 5. WIN — Ravensteyn Pharma NV (Belgium, compliance-driven)

**Date:** 15 May 2026 · **Deal size:** €55k ACV · **Attendees:** Lien Van Craen (L&D Director, champion/EB), Dries Vermeersch (IT/Security, blocker), Elke Van der Meiren (LearnLoop AE)

GMP retraining deadline was the trigger — Lien had a hard date from her quality team and no unified way to prove who'd actually completed the refresher training versus who'd just clicked through it. Deal moved fast for the first three weeks, then stalled two weeks on IT. Dries wanted specifics on SSO (Azure AD) and data residency before anything went further — standard blocker profile, but he was thorough about it, wanted the DPA and the hosting answer in writing before he'd say yes internally. Once we sent the EU-hosting confirmation and walked through the SSO setup live, he flipped from blocker to advocate in the same call, which surprised Lien as much as us.

**Buyer quotes:**
- "The moment you showed us the data stays in the EU and SSO works with our existing Azure AD, IT stopped blocking and started helping." — Dries Vermeersch
- "We had a compliance deadline breathing down our necks. We could not afford a six-month IT review." — Lien Van Craen
- "SSO and residency — that's basically the whole conversation for us." — Dries Vermeersch

**Next steps:** Signed, DPA countersigned. Kickoff early June. Lien open to being a reference for compliance-driven Benelux deals — flag for marketing.
