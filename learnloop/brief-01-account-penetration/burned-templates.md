# Burned Templates — the current outbound (0.4% reply rate)

> **Fictional.** These are the generic sequences LearnLoop's 3 SDRs are running today across the DACH list. They are the anti-patterns your workflow has to beat. Each is realistically bad — read them, then note *why* they fail. The 50 DACH accounts have already seen variations of these, so the list is partly burned. "Good" is not a nicer version of these; it's a different category of email.

---

## Template A — The "Hope this finds you well" cold open

**Subject:** Quick question, {{first_name}}

> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> Hope this email finds you well! My name is [SDR] and I'm reaching out from LearnLoop, a leading provider of innovative learning solutions.
>
> In today's fast-paced world, companies like {{company}} need to empower their teams and unlock their full potential. Our platform offers a seamless, end-to-end learning experience that drives real results.
>
> Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call this week to explore how we can help {{company}} on its learning journey?
>
> Best regards,
> [SDR]

**Why this fails:**
- Zero relevance — nothing here couldn't be sent to any company on earth. "{{company}}" is the only personalisation and it's a mail-merge token.
- Reads like every other SaaS email; pattern-matches straight to delete. Buyers filter this before finishing the first line.
- Banned-word soup: "empower / unlock / seamless / drives results / in today's fast-paced world." (These are literally on LearnLoop's own DON'T list — see the bible.)
- Asks for the buyer's time before giving them a single reason. The ask arrives before any value.

## Template B — The feature dump

**Subject:** LearnLoop — LMS + Skills + Content Marketplace

> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> I wanted to introduce you to LearnLoop, the all-in-one learning platform trusted by leading organisations.
>
> Here's what we offer:
> - ✅ A full-featured LMS with courses, learning paths, mobile and compliance tracking
> - ✅ A skills engine that maps who-can-do-what and flags gaps
> - ✅ 8,000+ off-the-shelf courses in our content marketplace
> - ✅ Seamless integrations, robust reporting, and enterprise-grade security
> - ✅ Best-in-class support
>
> We'd love to show you a demo. When are you free?
>
> Cheers,
> [SDR]

**Why this fails:**
- It's about LearnLoop, not the buyer. A feature list is not a reason to reply — it's a reason to feel sold to.
- No problem named. The buyer has to do the work of mapping features to their own pain; almost nobody will.
- Emoji-bulleted brochure copy signals "mass send." The champions on this list (e.g. an L&D Manager fighting SkillForge reporting) don't see themselves anywhere in it.
- "When are you free?" with no earned relevance = 0.4%.

## Template C — The fake-personalised LinkedIn-scrape

**Subject:** Loved your post, {{first_name}}!

> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> I saw you're the {{title}} at {{company}} — impressive! It's clear you're passionate about people and culture.
>
> At LearnLoop, we're on a mission to revolutionise how organisations approach learning and development. We help forward-thinking leaders like yourself build a culture of continuous growth.
>
> I'd love to pick your brain for 15 minutes. Are you the right person to speak to about learning initiatives at {{company}}?
>
> Warm regards,
> [SDR]

**Why this fails:**
- "Fake personalisation" — it name-drops the title from a data field and pretends that's research. Buyers can smell a scrape instantly.
- "Loved your post" when there is no specific post. If you can't quote it, don't claim it. This actively destroys trust.
- "Are you the right person to speak to?" tells the buyer you did zero homework on the buying committee. It offloads your qualification work onto them.
- "Revolutionise / forward-thinking / like yourself" — flattery as a substitute for relevance.

## Template D — The generic follow-up nudge

**Subject:** Re: Quick question, {{first_name}}

> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> Just floating this back to the top of your inbox in case you missed my last email!
>
> I know you're busy, but I really think LearnLoop could be a great fit for {{company}}. Even a quick "not now" would be helpful so I know where we stand.
>
> Looking forward to hearing from you either way.
>
> Thanks,
> [SDR]

**Why this fails:**
- Adds no new information — a bump with nothing behind it. If the first email had no reason to reply, repeating it doesn't create one.
- "I know you're busy" + guilt ("even a quick 'not now'") makes the buyer's silence the SDR's problem. It's needy, not compelling.
- No signal, no proof, no relevance — same category as A/B/C, just later in the sequence. Follow-ups this empty are why the list is burned.

---

## The pattern across all four

Generic personalisation, feature-dumping, banned words, and asking for time before earning it. Every one of these could have been sent to any of the 50 accounts unchanged — which is exactly the problem. **Good outbound is account-specific and signal-driven: it proves you looked, names a real pain the buyer already feels, and makes the reply easy.** That's the bar in `baseline-and-targets.md`.
