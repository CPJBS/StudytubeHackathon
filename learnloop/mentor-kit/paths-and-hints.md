# MENTOR-ONLY — Paths & Hints (per brief)

> For mentors. Not for participants. **The core mentor job in one line:** good looks like a team that has *gone through the assets, identified a path, can reason about why, and built a solution around it.* There is no right path. Grade the evidence trail, never the path choice.
>
> How to use the hints: escalate gently. H1 is a question, H2-H3 point at an asset, H4 is direct. Stop escalating the moment the team starts moving — the goal is that they find the path, not that you hand it over.

---

## Brief 01 — The Signal Room

**Seeded paths (all defensible, different top-3s):**

| Path | The read | Evidence trail | Its blind spot |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regulation / deadline | Hard dates = urgency (live RFP, FINMA requirement, LMS end-of-life) | `signal-feed.md` rfp/compliance/tech_change items | A deadline is not budget, and not access |
| Leadership change | New CHRO/CPO with a mandate = open window | feed leadership items; `what-the-org-believes.md` | The new-leader decoys (interim caretaker, cost-cutting mandate) |
| Competitor displacement | Failing incumbent = wedge (SkillForge/Cognita/Bloomwork pain) | feed competitor items, renewal windows, champion LinkedIn posts | Complaints are not switching intent; rip-and-replace is slow |
| Warm path | Relationship access beats signal freshness; every past win was warm | `warm-paths.md`, `win-loss-call-notes.md`, the 0.4% cold baseline | Some warm accounts have no live trigger; references spend once |
| Burn-aware | Respect the send-log: dead is dead, hand-raisers first | `send-log.csv`, `berlin-pod-call-notes.md` | Past burn is not destiny; ignores fresh signals |

Note: ACC-002 surfaces on almost every path (that is fine, it is the shared anchor). The scaffolds disagree on purpose: Tier A contains decoys, the pod appendix contains misreads, contact-signals covers ambiguous accounts. A team that *disputes* a scaffold with evidence is doing the work.

**Hints for stuck teams:**
- **H1:** "Which Monday-morning question is your system answering, and for whom? Frieda can do eight calls a day."
- **H2:** "You have read the market feed. Have you read what the company already knows? `win-loss-call-notes.md` says how deals actually close here."
- **H3:** "The pod's appendix is their judgment, not ground truth. Where do you agree with them? Where not, and why?"
- **H4:** "Pick any two accounts and argue both sides: why hot, why not. That argument is your scoring model. Now write it down and apply it to all 50."

## Brief 02 — The Brand Voice Machine

**Seeded paths (all defensible, different machines):**

| Path | The machine | Evidence trail | Its blind spot |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rules-first gate | Codify the guide into explicit rules; score and explain per rule | `voice-guide.md` DO/DON'T + banned lists | Misses subtle drift: "zero banned words, still generic" |
| Exemplar matcher | Voice = "sounds like the corpus"; nearest-exemplar + what differs | `on-brand-corpus.md` (20 pieces), `gold-standard.md` | Can flag, struggles to *explain*; risks mimicry of one style |
| Author profiles | Model each of the six authors' characteristic drift; per-author correction | `author-samples/`, `off-brand-stack.md` | Thin data per author; drift is not always author-shaped |
| Transcreation-first | The language crossing is the engine; EN checking is the easy part | `de-launch-memo.md`, guide's DE/ES adaptation sections | Can neglect the production line while perfecting German |
| Producer-first | Center on brief-in → asset-out; the gate is one step on the belt | `asset-briefs-from-the-pod.md` | A weak gate ships confident drift at scale |
| Config voice-OS | Rules, registers, formats all as data; a new language = a new config | README's "French is configuration, not a rebuild" | Abstraction eats the afternoon; demo risk |

**Hints for stuck teams:**
- **H1:** "Run stack draft #8 through your checker. Zero banned words. Is it on-voice? What is your checker not measuring yet?"
- **H2:** "The guide is one way in. Could you define the voice as 'sounds like these 20 pieces' instead? What would you measure then?"
- **H3:** "Put the freelance German translation next to the guide's DE section. What exactly died in translation? Name three things."
- **H4:** "Your gate works; your belt is empty. Take the first request in `asset-briefs-from-the-pod.md` and produce the asset, then run it through your own gate."

## Brief 03 — The Account Penetration Machine

**Seeded paths (all defensible, different first accounts and doors):**

| Path | The motion | Evidence trail | Its blind spot |
|---|---|---|---|
| Signal-first | Freshest trigger gets the next touch, multi-threaded | `baseline-and-targets.md` starter signals, accounts.csv | Signal-rich accounts may be burned or gate-kept |
| Hand-raiser first | Four people replied positively last quarter; nobody followed up | `send-log.csv` positive rows | Only four accounts; not a whole-quarter motion |
| Warm path / reference spend | Spend the nine beachhead relationships deliberately | `warm-paths.md`, every win in `win-loss-call-notes.md` | Scarce, one-shot; most paths are thin or hearsay |
| Event-anchored | Show up where buyers already are (HR-Kongress, Stammtisch, L&D-Slack) | `warm-paths.md` venue map | Slow cadence; hard to demo as a "machine" |
| Clean-list first | Seven accounts never touched; start where the well is not poisoned | `send-log.csv` untouched contacts | Untouched often means lower-tier fit |
| Channel-switch | 20+ opens, zero replies = interested, wrong door; switch to call/LinkedIn | `send-log.csv` opens-vs-replies pattern | Opens are a weak signal; risks over-reading pixels |

**Hints for stuck teams:**
- **H1:** "How many of your first ten touches are email? What did email earn last quarter? The number is in the baseline."
- **H2:** "Four people replied positively last quarter and nobody followed up. Have you found them yet?"
- **H3:** "Who in `warm-paths.md` can open a door your sequence cannot? And what does spending that favor cost, since it spends once?"
- **H4:** "Take one account through the whole machine by hand: committee, doors, timing, asset briefs. Then automate what you just did."
