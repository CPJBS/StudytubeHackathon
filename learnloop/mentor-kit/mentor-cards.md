# MENTOR-ONLY — What Good Looks Like (coaching cards)

> One card per brief. Use while roaming: these are the five checks per brief, plus the traps teams fall into and the nudge that unsticks them. No formal scoring — the room votes "closest to ship-ready" — but these keep coaching consistent across mentors.

---

## Card 1 · REACH (Account Penetration)

**The five checks**
1. Only-this-account test: could the email go to any of the 50 unchanged? Fail.
2. Repeatability: does the machine do the *next* account live? (Holdout: `accounts-holdout.csv`.)
3. Multi-threading: buyer + champion + blocker, coordinated angles — not one email × 3.
4. Signal citation: every touch quotes a real, dated trigger. "If you can't quote it, don't claim it."
5. Voice + honesty: zero banned words; credible lift story vs 0.4%.

**Common traps → nudges**
- Writing one great email by hand → "Beautiful. Now make your tool produce it for ACC-007 without you."
- Ignoring the call notes → "What did the works council kill in Frieda's call? Is your email about to repeat it?"
- Using every signal at once → "Which ONE signal makes *now* the moment? Lead with that."
- Generic German → "Sie-form? Proof attached? Would a Nuremberg L&D director read past line one?"

**Demo bar (public promise):** outreach so specific the room forgets it was AI-assisted — then it does the next one too.

## Card 2 · SPEAK (Brand Voice)

**The five checks**
1. Catches subtle drift (the 3 zero-banned-word drafts in the stack), not just buzzword bingo.
2. Rewrites read like `gold-standard.md`, not rule-compliant mush.
3. Every change explained: rule, why, before/after.
4. Travels: DE/ES are transcreations (register, proof-hunger, warmth) — beats the flat translation in `de-launch-memo.md`.
5. Consistent: same draft twice → same verdict.

**Ground truth (do not reveal):** author-samples: Marcus + Sofia on-voice; Priya, Daniel, Tom, Hannah drifted. Off-brand stack: all 10 drifted (3 obvious / 4 medium / 3 subtle — labels in HTML comments). `unseen-drafts.md` holdouts: 1 Hannah + 2 Daniel on-voice; 3 Sofia (medium) + 4 Tom (subtle) drifted.

**Common traps → nudges**
- Banned-word grep as the whole checker → "Run it on off-brand draft #8. Zero banned words. Still beige. Why?"
- Rewrites that are compliant but dead → "Read it next to the gold standard. Which would you keep reading?"
- Literal translation → "Back-translate your German. Does the claim still lead? Did the aside survive?"

**Demo bar (public promise):** ugly draft in, on-brand out, every change explained — then prove it travels to Madrid and Berlin.

## Card 3 · SENSE (Signal Room)

**The five checks**
1. Traps caught, not narrated: channel names normalized; March quarantined; funnel-vs-LinkedIn reconciled; loud ≠ valuable; blank ≠ zero.
2. 2–4 insights with evidence + confidence levels (not ten charts).
3. Honest limits: names what the data can't answer + one thing to instrument.
4. Alive: answers "what happened here?" on the spot (use `fresh-questions.md`; late drop `late-data-drop.csv` at ~15:30).
5. Three-minute VP read; they'd open it Monday.

**Answer key:** `../brief-03-signal-room/contradictions-and-redherrings.md` (incl. the Jul 13 German-beachhead addendum).

**Common traps → nudges**
- "LinkedIn is small" or "March was a breakout" → "Group by channel again — how many spellings of LinkedIn do you have?" / "What did *activated* do in March while sessions doubled?"
- Chart-dump dashboard → "Point at it and ask: so what do we DO? If the tool can't say, it's a screensaver."
- Quoting the loudest review → "Is that a pattern or an anecdote? How many of the 21 say it?"
- Ignoring the beliefs file → "Five executives disagree. Your tool's first job is to referee. Who's right?"

**Demo bar (public promise):** something LearnLoop would open again on Monday — a living view they can interrogate, not a screenshot.
