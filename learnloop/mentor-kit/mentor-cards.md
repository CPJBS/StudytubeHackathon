# MENTOR-ONLY — What Good Looks Like (coaching cards) · canon v3

> One card per brief. Use while roaming: five checks per brief, the traps teams fall into, and the nudge that unsticks them. No formal scoring — the room votes "closest to ship-ready" — but these keep coaching consistent across mentors.
> v3 scoping: SENSE = signal triage · REACH = signal→action motion · SPEAK = content production at scale.

---

## Card 3 · SENSE (The Signal Room — triage)

**The five checks**
1. **Noise caught.** Ground truth: 8 accounts have *only* a noise decoy in the feed — **ACC-021, 024, 029, 031, 034, 039, 040, 046** (capex funding, call-center hiring sprees, irrelevant expansions). A triage ranking any of these hot got fooled.
2. **Quiet accounts handled.** **ACC-001, 010, 023, 041, 043, 045** have zero feed items — deprioritised *with reasoning*, not forgotten. (Sharp teams notice ACC-001 is feed-quiet but rich in `tech_signals` and the call notes — the feed isn't the only source.)
3. **Hot list justified.** Every hot account cites its signals and the why-for-LearnLoop. The pod's hand-tagged appendix treated as judgment to verify, not truth.
4. **Calibration depth (the old traps, now bonus material).** Channel-name chaos, the March outlier, review patterns — teams that fold "what converts for us" into the heat logic have gone deeper. Answer key: `../brief-01-signal-room/contradictions-and-redherrings.md`.
5. **It's alive.** At ~15:30 hand them `late-signal-drop.md`: ACC-010 must move UP, ACC-023 must NOT move, ACC-043 → "watch, don't act." And it answers "why is X above Y?" with evidence.

**Common traps → nudges**
- Ranking by signal *count* → "Volume isn't heat. Which single signal would make YOU call tomorrow morning?"
- Trusting the pod's tags → "Their judgment, not ground truth. Spot-check one — do you agree?"
- Skipping calibration → "Right now this is generic lead scoring. What makes an account hot *for LearnLoop specifically*?"
- Chart-dump → "Nobody at LearnLoop needs an 11th dashboard. Where's the WHO and the WHY?"

**Demo bar:** fresh signal in → "who moved up, and why?" — answered with evidence.

## Card 1 · REACH (Account Penetration — motion)

**The five checks**
1. **The play matches the signal.** Swap the signal and the plan should change. If it doesn't, it's a generic sequence with a signal pasted on.
2. **Committee worked as a committee.** Buyer + champion + blocker get different doors and coordinated angles, in a deliberate order — not one email × 3.
3. **Routing is real.** `send-log.csv` respected (no email to burned/bounced contacts); at least one account routed to a *human* (call brief for the pod); warm paths and events used as doors, deliberately.
4. **Asset briefs SPEAK can produce from.** Audience, signal, angle, format, language — and no copy. If the brief contains the email text, they did SPEAK's job.
5. **Repeatable.** Second account through the machine, live. (Holdout: `accounts-holdout.csv`.)

**Common traps → nudges**
- Writing the copy → "That's the Brand Voice team's job. Order it: what's the brief?"
- Ignoring the send-log → "This contact received 43 emails last quarter. Why is your plan emailing him?"
- One-door plans → "Everything here is email. What would Frieda do with this account?"
- Waiting for SENSE → "The starter signals are in baseline-and-targets.md. Swap in the real triage at the reconnect."

**Demo bar:** one hot account + signal in → complete attack plan out (play, committee, channels, timing, asset briefs) — then the next account.

## Card 2 · SPEAK (Brand Voice — production line)

**The five checks**
1. **Produces from a brief.** The stand-ins in `asset-briefs-from-the-pod.md`: right audience, right angle, right format, right language and register. A checker alone is half the machine now — the line must *ship assets*.
2. **Catches subtle drift** (3 zero-banned-word drafts in the stack), not just buzzword bingo — and the rewrites read like `gold-standard.md`, not rule-compliant mush.
3. **Every voice decision explained** — rule, why, before/after.
4. **Travels.** DE/ES are transcreations (register, proof-hunger, warmth) — beats the flat translation in `de-launch-memo.md`. Stretch: derives French register rules from how the guide adapts DE/ES, states its assumptions, ships one asset.
5. **Consistent.** Same input twice → same output judgment. A system, not a dice roll.

**Ground truth (do not reveal):** author-samples: Marcus + Sofia on-voice; Priya, Daniel, Tom, Hannah drifted. Off-brand stack: all 10 drifted (3 obvious / 4 medium / 3 subtle — labels in HTML comments, which are STRIPPED from participant copies). `unseen-drafts.md` holdouts: 1 (Hannah) + 2 (Daniel) on-voice; 3 (Sofia, medium) + 4 (Tom, subtle) drifted.

**Common traps → nudges**
- Checker-only build → "Has your line actually *shipped* an asset from a brief yet? That's the job."
- Banned-word grep as the checker → "Run it on stack draft #8. Zero banned words. Still generic. Why?"
- Literal translation → "Back-translate your German. Does the claim still lead? Did the aside survive?"
- Ignoring the ICP's language → "The quote bank is how buyers actually talk. Does your asset sound like it's for them?"

**Demo bar:** asset brief in → on-voice German asset out, decisions explained — then a language with no guide.

## Cross-brief (any table)

- "If another team had to pick this up cold, what would you hand them?" (ask from hour one — it primes the reconnect without revealing it)
- "Which shared-world file haven't you used yet?" (win-loss notes and the quote bank are gold most teams skip)
- The chain, if a team loses the plot: **SENSE says who and why now → REACH picks the play and orders content → SPEAK produces it → the send goes out → replies feed SENSE.**
- Credits anxiety → "The cap is generous. Stop rationing, start building."
