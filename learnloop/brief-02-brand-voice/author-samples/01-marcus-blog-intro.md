**Title: Your completion rate is lying to you**

Last quarter, one of our customers ran a compliance course. 94% completion. The VP People forwarded the dashboard to her board like a trophy.

Six weeks later, the same team failed the exact audit the training was supposed to prevent.

Nobody had done anything wrong. The course was finished. The boxes were ticked. The dashboard was green. And none of it meant the skill had landed — because completion measures whether someone clicked "next," not whether they can do the job differently on Monday.

This is the quiet lie at the center of most L&D reporting. We track the thing that's easy to count and call it proof. Then we defend the budget with it, right up until reality sends the bill.

Loop was built to answer the question completion can't: did the capability actually change, and is it being used? That's a harder number to move. It's also the only one that survives contact with your board.

Here's what tracking capability instead of completion actually looks like — and why it made three of our customers kill half their course catalog.
