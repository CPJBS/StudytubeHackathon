# Author: Sofia — Product announcement
*Content type: Feature launch / product update post*

---

**We're killing the completion badge. Here's why.**

Starting today, Loop no longer shows a completion percentage on the main dashboard. We moved it. On purpose.

We did it because that number was the first thing every customer looked at, and it was the least useful thing on the screen. A 90% completion rate tells you people clicked through. It tells you nothing about whether the sales team can now handle the objection the course was built to teach.

So we replaced it. The dashboard now leads with **capability coverage** — for each skill your org needs, how many of the people who need it can actually demonstrate it. Not "have they watched the video." Can they do the thing.

The completion number still exists. You'll find it two clicks in, where a supporting metric belongs. Some of you will hate that we moved it. One customer already emailed to say we'd "buried the number my CFO asks about."

Fair. But the CFO's real question isn't "did they finish." It's "did the money change anything." Capability coverage answers that one. Completion never could.

It's live now. Log in and look at the top of your dashboard — it's a different question than it was yesterday.
