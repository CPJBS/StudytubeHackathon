**Audience:** DACH manufacturing prospects (and secondary: L&D directors at scale-ups and mid-market). Economic buyers, champions, and skeptics who've sat through a compliance training rollout and felt the gap between completion rates and actual behavior change.

**The signal:** Q3 push into manufacturing compliance training (safety-critical, works-council-sensitive, high-stakes audits). We need a provable contrarian hook to cut through content noise and earn 90 seconds of attention from a CMO or CHRO.

**The angle:** Completion theater is real in safety-critical industries. Highlight the exact mechanism: 100% compliance-training completion + 0% change in actual safety behavior = a lie the metrics tell. Contrarian, provable, and owned by the reader's lived experience. Land with what the alternative actually means (skill verification, not just seat-time).

**Format + channel:** LinkedIn post (500–700 words). Native, no external links. Tone: confident and slightly uncomfortable (the reader should feel seen). Sprinkle 1–2 specific numbers if available.

**Language + register:** English. Direct, short sentences. Contractions welcome. One dry aside. Lead with the claim or number, not the preamble.

**What NOT to do:**  
- No banned words: `empower`, `unlock`, `seamless`, `leverage`, `robust`, `best-in-class`, `drive results`, `in today's world`.

- No "In today's fast-paced business environment…" windup. First sentence should stand alone.
- No feature list. No "LearnLoop does X, Y, Z." Say what changes, not what we have.
- No AI-cadence tricolons or "X. Full stop." rhythm-chasing.
- Corporate-neutral tone is a fail. This should sound like a person who works in L&D, not a corporate newsletter.

**Reference:** Voice Guide, principles 1–4 and DO/DON'T sections.

## 3. Landing-Page Section: Falkenrode Logistik Reference Story

**Filed by:** Lukas Brandt (Berlin pod)  
**Date:** 2026-07-13  

**Audience:** Logistics prospects (150–1,500 employees, mid-market, Europe). Primary reader: L&D Manager or People Ops Lead. Secondary reader: Works Council representative (DE context: employee data-protection concerns are real and active in account decisions). Economic buyer (HR/People leadership) will see this later; write for the operations-level reader first.

**The signal:** Falkenrode Logistik (Hamburg, 1,100 employees, logistics) is our canonical DACH beachhead reference. Landing-page story for the logistics vertical, Q3/Q4 push. Works Council sensitivity is the blocker in every logistics deal — any mention of "monitoring" or "tracking individuals" kills it. Frame as *capability visibility for teams*, not surveillance.

**The angle:** Show how a 1,100-person logistics operation scaled training accountability without triggering works-council resistance. Lead with what the teams gained (clarity on who can do what, faster onboarding, reduced checklists). Bury the "we knew who was ready" insight deep — let it sit as subtext. The real story: Falkenrode moved from seat-time theater to actual readiness. Proof: onboarding cycle, team-capability clarity, compliance audit outcome. Not: "we tracked everyone."

**Format + channel:** German landing-page section (600–800 words). Modular; fits a case-study flow or vertical-market page. Tone: case-study warm, not sales-brochure. Conversational but precise (German section guidance: complete thoughts, no clipping).

**Language + register:** German, Sie-form. Formal. Specific numbers (1,100 employees, Hamburg, weeks saved, teams affected). No assumptions about works-council reader; assume they're smart and skeptical of data-collection claims.

**What NOT to do:**  
- **Avoid any language suggesting individual tracking or surveillance.** No "wir können nun sehen, was jeder Mitarbeiter kann." No "Überwachung," no "Monitoring von Mitarbeitern." Reframe as team-capability visibility, department readiness, role requirements.
- No corporate filler (`ganzheitliche Lösung`, `nahtlose Integration`, `maßgeschneidert`). Precision and mechanism beat adjectives.
- No hedging. Say the outcome clearly: "Onboarding-Zyklen sanken von X auf Y Wochen" — not "können potenziell sanken."
- No feature list (Loop does X, Learn does Y). Say what changed operationally.
- Don't mention works council explicitly (reads as defensive). Instead, lead with team/operational benefits — the works-council concern dissolves if the story is about team capability, not compliance surveillance.
- No Denglish; German is the language. "Skills" in German context is acceptable as a loanword, but default to German equivalents (Fähigkeiten, Kompetenzen, Kenntnisse).

**Reference:** Voice Guide, German section guidance: Sie-Form, more precise less punchy, contrarian but earned, no German corporate filler. Additionally: structure around what the *teams* gained, not what management could now see.
