What we've learned so far
Cost. Cheaper to run than in 2020, but less than you'd think. Tokens and infrastructure add up fast.
Hiring. Harder, honestly. A hard skill you can test for in an interview. Curiosity, bias to action, comfort with AI are much harder to spot. So we hire for mindset and rent the specialist skills when we need them.
Speed. Faster overall. But a system costs time before it saves any, and people get impatient. That impatience is what threatens the build.
Talent. This is the real win. A good system lifts everyone. The generalists who love to build but never had the deep skills? Give them AI and something to build in, and they get dangerous.

If you're growing a marketing career right now, here's what I'd do.

01
Talk to actual customers. When did you last speak to one? It was always the job.
02
Develop your judgment. You can't catch what's wrong on a market you only know through dashboards.
03
Make AI your daily operating system. Rebuild one recurring workflow a month. What did you stop doing by hand?
04
Plan your next step, not your next three. The roles you'd aim for in step three don't exist yet.
